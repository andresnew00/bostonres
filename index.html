The Problem

Animals in Boston need help every day. Not just in December.

You care. You've seen the posts, shared the stories, maybe even lit a candle for a dog you never met. But something keeps getting in the way of doing more.

📅

The timing problem

Most donations to ARL arrive in the final two weeks of December. But animals get sick, hurt, and lost in January, March, and August too. The need never takes a holiday.

🏠

The housing crisis hits pets hard

29% of surrenders at ARL in 2024 came from housing-related reasons. A family loses their apartment. The landlord says no pets. A good owner makes an impossible choice.

💸

Zero government funding

Not a dime of public funding supports this work. Every vaccine, every surgery, every rescue comes from people who decided animals matter. People exactly like you.

🩺

Vet care costs are rising

The cost of owning a dog is now $1,400 a year, nearly double what it was in 2010. Families who love their pets are forced to choose between care and keeping them.

Why ARL

More than a shelter. A full system built to keep pets and people together.

ARL goes far beyond finding homes for animals. We bring veterinary care directly into communities. We run mobile spay and neuter clinics across Cape Cod and Eastern Massachusetts. We keep families together through food pantries and temporary pet housing when things get hard.

When you give monthly, you fund all of it. Adoption. Emergency rescue. Community vet clinics. Law enforcement for animal cruelty. A free pet behavior helpline. 125 years of work, with no signs of stopping.

Charity Navigator gives ARL 4 stars. Candid gives us Platinum transparency status. Great Nonprofits named us top-rated in 2026. This is an organization that puts your money to work.
